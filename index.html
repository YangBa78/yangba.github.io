About

I am a final-year Ph.D. student in Data Science, Analytics and Engineering at Arizona State University. I previously earned my M.S. in Business Analytics from the University of Connecticut, where I was co-supervised by Rong Pan and Michelle V. Mancenido, and worked with the DHS Center for Accelerating Operational Efficiency (DHS-CAOE).

My research interests span machine learning and generative AI, with a particular focus on large language models (LLMs) and multimodal learning. I study synthetic data generation and evaluation to understand how generative and foundation models learn, represent, and generalize across modalities. My current work explores the benefits and risks of synthetic data, improves LLM calibration, and enhances multimodal performance through latent space alignment. More broadly, I aim to advance responsible AI systems through synthetic data, model interpretability, and statistical data analysis.
